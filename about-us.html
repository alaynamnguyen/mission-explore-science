WHAT WE DO

What is Mission: Xplore Science?

Mission: Xplore Science is a nonprofit organization that aims to educate, motivate, and inspire the next generation of complex thinkers by introducing students to citizen science initiatives in the classroom. Our user-friendly platform empowers kids to discover different scientific branches including astronomy, biology, and technology, all while progressing research projects around the world. Students engage in fun and interactive data collection and analysis by following our comprehensive guides, becoming a valuable part of the global scientific community.

What is Citizen Science?

Citizen science is a government-sponsored research project made available to the public. People around the globe have access to these projects and can readily contribute from anywhere, anytime, to a wide variety of scientific research going on.

What Do We Do?

Our mission is to help elementary students interact with the scientific community while also staying on track with their curriculum. We compile comprehensive, grade-level citizen science projects across six different subjects: Astronomy, Biology, Earth Science, Nature, Oceanography, and Physics.

Students: Which Projects Should I Do?

Each of the projects have a brief summary which gives you an idea of what might interest you. They also come with a difficult level, with 5 stars being the most difficult and 1 star being the easiest. If this is your first time doing a citizen science project, try starting with 1-star projects. Once you choose a project, read the step-by-step instructions and follow the link. Some instructions have a mark, meaning you need to get an adult for that step. All of these options have a “Recommended Number of Students” tab. Beware that some projects may have you work in partners, groups, or as a whole class.

For Teachers

Along with the information provided in the Student Information, under each project there is also information about which types of curriculums each research aligns with. Although citizen science projects are certainly no substitute for classroom learning time, each project also comes with either worksheets, learning guides, or slide presentations which may be a good introduction to a course curriculum or supplemental learning materials.
